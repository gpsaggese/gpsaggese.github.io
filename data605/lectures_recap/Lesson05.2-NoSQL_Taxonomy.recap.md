1. Analyze a specific application in a real-world context where each of the five
   database types (relational, key-value, document, columnar, graph) could be
   effectively applied. Explain your reasoning for each choice and how the
   characteristics of the database type align with the application’s needs.

2. Compare and contrast the strengths and weaknesses of relational databases and
   document stores. In what scenarios would you prefer to use one over the
   other, and why might a developer opt for a document store despite the
   availability of relational databases?

3. Discuss the significance of the CAP theorem in the context of database
   design. How does the CAP theorem influence the choice of a database system
   for applications requiring high availability? Provide examples of situations
   where a trade-off between consistency and availability must be made.

4. Reflect on the implications of schema flexibility in databases like document
   stores versus the rigid structure of relational databases. How does schema
   flexibility impact data retrieval and application development, especially in
   dynamic environments where data characteristics may frequently evolve?
