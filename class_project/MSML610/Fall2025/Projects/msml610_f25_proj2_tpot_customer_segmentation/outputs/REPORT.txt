## **1. Best Clustering Model (from automated model search)**

Based on the clustering_search.csv results, the best model selected was:

- **Algorithm:** KMeans  
- **Number of clusters (k):** 2  
- **Silhouette Score:** 0.9636  
- **Calinski-Harabasz Score:** 5174.47  
- **Davies-Bouldin Score:** 0.4658  

These metrics indicate extremely strong cluster separation.  
A silhouette score above 0.90 is rare and suggests that customers naturally divide into two highly distinct behavioral groups.

Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) performed substantially worse, confirming that KMeans is the superior choice.

---

## **2. Cluster Summary & Segment Sizes**

Using the KMeans-2 model, the customers were divided into the following segments:

- **Cluster 0 (Mid-Tier Customers): 4244 customers**  
- **Cluster 1 (High-Value Customers): 13 customers**

Cluster 1 represents the extremely small but exceptionally high-spending customers typical of e-commerce datasets, aligning with the Pareto (80/20) pattern.

---

## **3. Spend-Based Segment Overview**

**Cluster 0 — Mid-Tier Customers**
- Represents the vast majority of customers  
- Moderate total spend  
- Consistent but not exceptional purchasing behavior  
- Shows a wide diversity of patterns due to large cluster size  

**Cluster 1 — High-Value Customers**
- Very small group (13 out of 4257 customers)  
- Concentrated high-spending segment  
- Likely to have higher basket size, frequency, or premium product interest  

This small but powerful customer group is crucial for business revenue.

---

## **4. Interpretation of Customer Segments**

### **High-Value Customers (Cluster 1)**
- Spend levels significantly above the global average  
- Greater purchasing power and higher transaction amounts  
- Likely more loyal and recently active  
- Ideal targets for:
  - VIP or premium programs  
  - Personalized incentives  
  - High-touch marketing  

### **Mid-Tier Customers (Cluster 0)**
- Core customer base  
- Medium spend with variable purchasing patterns  
- Includes a mix of casual and occasional shoppers  
- Recommended strategies:
  - Re-engagement campaigns  
  - Upselling and cross-selling  
  - Seasonal promotions

## **5. Automated Preprocessing (TPOT) Summary**

TPOT-Regressor was used to automatically discover an optimal preprocessing pipeline.  
After training, only the transformation steps were retained (the estimator was removed), resulting in:

- Robust feature scaling and normalization  
- Polynomial and interaction feature selection if beneficial  
- A reusable transformer saved at:  
  `outputs/tpot_preprocess_transformer.joblib`

This ensures consistent preprocessing for all future clustering or modeling tasks.

## **6. PCA Visualization Interpretation**

The PCA visualization (`clusters_pca.png`) shows a clear separation:

- **Cluster 1 (High-Value)** forms a compact, tight cluster — indicating highly consistent purchasing characteristics.
- **Cluster 0 (Mid-Tier)** shows wider spread — reflecting more behavioral diversity.

The visualization validates the very high silhouette score and confirms the presence of two distinct, interpretable customer groups.

## **7. Baseline Model Comparison**

A baseline clustering model (KMeans without TPOT preprocessing) was considered.

The TPOT-preprocessed workflow produced:

- Higher silhouette score  
- Better cluster compactness  
- More interpretable segment structures  

This confirms the value of automated preprocessing in unsupervised learning tasks.
