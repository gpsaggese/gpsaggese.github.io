Hospital System Expands Emergency Department Services

The Regional Medical Center announced expansion of its emergency department facilities to accommodate growing patient volumes. The project includes adding twelve new treatment rooms and upgrading diagnostic equipment.

Hospital administrators report that emergency visits have increased by 20 percent over the past three years. The expansion aims to reduce patient wait times and improve treatment quality.

Construction will take approximately 14 months and is expected to begin in the second quarter. The hospital secured funding through a combination of state grants and private donations. Total project cost is estimated at 8.5 million dollars.

The new facilities will include advanced imaging technology and dedicated areas for trauma cases. Staff will receive additional training on new equipment prior to opening.

Patients and their families expressed support for the expansion during a community forum. The hospital committed to maintaining service continuity throughout construction.
