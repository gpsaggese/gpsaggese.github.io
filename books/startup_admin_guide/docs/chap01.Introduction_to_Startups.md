## Vision, Mission, and Strategic Planning

* Startups are special
- Small: 3-10 people
- Nimble: like the Navy Seals, no committee
- Un-constrained: there are no sacred cows
- Courages: startups take risks, big companies found something that works and
  don't want to change
- Innovative: small set of people starting with a blank slate can come up with
  something completely new

- Fragile: a big company can easily crash a large company

* Big-company vs startup
- In a big company: responsibility diffuse, everyone has a little corner
- In a startup: everyone feels responsible

- In a big company: you must defend your cash cows
- In a startup: you must put everything on the line

- In a big company: incremental improvements to the working product
- In a startup: innovate or die

- In a big company: management is important
- In a startup: management is not necessary (everyone knows what everyone is
  doing)

- In a big company: default alive (if they don't do anything, they survive)
- In a startup: default dead (everyone loses their job if startup doesn't
  innovate)

* Why are startups so valuable?
- Most economic growth comes from innovation
- Most innovation comes from startups
- Many Google products came from acquisition
  - DoubleClick
  - YouTube
  - Android
  - Google Maps

- In a world of declining growth and interest rates, innovation is extremely
  valuable
  - How much percent return I will give you if you take my money and do
    something with it
  - It is a measure of how productive can be to loan out money
  - How many good ideas vs how many money
    - Right now there is a lot of money, but few ideas

- In the last 800 years nominal interest rates have consistently going down
  (with cycles) but the trend is from 15% to 0%

- US VC investments went from $50b in 2012 to $300b in 2022 (6x in 10 years)
  - The seed round went from $0.5m to $2.5m

- If you are successful at a startup, the rewards are immense
  - But you probably won't be successful (and that's ok)

* 5 big steps to build a startup
- Learn the culture, products, and history of the sector
- Find great co-founders and team
- Come up with a great idea
- Validate that the idea is great
- Raise money for it

* Step 1: Learn!
- Before building anything learn as much as possible
- Talk to people
- Join a web3 startup
  - It's the best education you can get
- Read, play, go to meet-ups and hackathons
- Be technical
