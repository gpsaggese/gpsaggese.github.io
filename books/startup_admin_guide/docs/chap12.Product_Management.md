## Product Development and Market Fit

* Product
- A tool or utility that solves a need
- Sometimes the problem solves a problem that it is different than what it is
  actually solving
  - E.g., Facebook "make the world more connected" vs "need of humans to show
    off" and "do something when you are bored"
- The best product usually doesn't win
  - What's difficult is how to acquire users
  - Markets are pretty unfair because there are companies with unfair advantages
    and are willing to use it

* Needs
- You need to understand which of the 7 deadly sins your product is catering to

- There are few human drivers
  - Lust (vs chastity)
  - Gluttony (vs temperance)
  - Greed (vs generosity)
  - Sloth (vs diligence)
  - Wrath (vs patience)
  - Envy (vs gratitude)
  - Pride (vs humility)
- The human drivers do not change

- Humans spend a lot of money to signal to other humans that they are "fit"
  (your DNA is great and your offspring will be fantastic)
  - Luxury goods
  - In the real world, it's through clothes, the restaurants you go to
    (that's one of the values of Instagram)
  - NFTs v1 can solve the same need (e.g., the Bored Ape)
  - NFTs v2: NFTs are a tech solution to the fact that your most important
    possessions are all digital (e.g., photos, an entry in a DB to represent that
    you went to Berkeley)

* Product-market fit
- = you have something that is so excellent that the market is going to pull it
  out of you
- = a product that the market demands so much that the builders of the product
  will succeed despite their best efforts to fail
- E.g., Twitter (they can barely keep the website up and they are worth $40B)
- Success is easy if you have product-market fit

* "Why Now?" is the most important question
- Markets are pretty efficients and they prevent a certain innovation from
  succeeding / existing for a reason
1) New technology
   - E.g., battery technology enabled electric cars
2) New user acquisition channel
   - E.g., search / SEO
   - Tokenomics
3) New business model
   - Advertising supports free content online
4) Customer behavior has shifted
   - People understand the consequences of permanent identity online ->
     ephemerality
   - Put your information online to make it searchable by others -> social
     networking 
5) New regulation
   - Obamacare

* How to do product validation?
- Deeply understand your market
- Deeply understand your user / human need
- Learn (aka fail) as quickly as possible
  - The company that moves is the one that moves faster
  - That's why startups win, it's because they can move and fail faster

- People think that success is a straight line
- In reality success is full of wrong turns
  - It's like failing all the times and feels terrible

* Founder-product and founder-market fit
- Because running a startup feels terrible, it's important that the founder has
  certain characteristics
- E.g., the best products are created by people who are obsessed with the
  problem, the solution, the market and NOT with the outcome
  - It needs to be the mission of your life

- Of the millions of people who could build this, why is it you that will build
  it?
- Of the millions of products you could build, why is this the only product that
  your life should be dedicated to?
  - E.g., Elon Musk is obsessed with space
