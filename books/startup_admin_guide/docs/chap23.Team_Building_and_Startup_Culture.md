## Finding and Working with Co-founders

* Step 2: Assemble a team
- Best team are comprised of friends
- The #1 cause of company failures is co-founder breakups
  - It's like divorce, it's horrible
- You shouldn't start a company together right away
  - Date before getting married
  - Start with a small project (e.g., a consulting gig)
  - Ideally do something under pressure (it's easy to get along when things are
    good, when things are horrible people change dramatically)
- Ideal co-founder is:
  - Somebody you trust (with your life)
  - Competent and resourceful (like James Bond in whichever situation finds a
    solution)
  - Complementary to you
    - If co-founders have the same skills and competence -> attrition ("I'm the
      expert on ...")
- Solo founder or 7?
  - More often to fail
  - 4 is too many (too much consensus building)
- Who's the CEO?
  - Uncomfortably decision and conversation
  - One needs to make a decision when there is a deadlock
- Also equity splits are tricky (depend on what they bring)
