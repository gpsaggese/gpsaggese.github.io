# 1, What the Heck Is EOS?

* EOS
- Entrepreneurial Operating System
- = the way a company organizes all of its human energy to
  - Solve problems
  - Meet
  - Plan
  - Prioritize
  - Communicate
  - Clarify roles
  - Lead
  - Manage

- Inconsistency in company operating systems leads to
  - Poor communication
  - Dysfunction
  - Employees feeling frustrated and confused about the priorities
  - The company not realizing its full potential

- If everyone does things in their own way the organization can't work
  - Meet when they want
  - Set their priorities
  - Use different terminology

- When people move from project to project
  - A new system needs to be learned
  - Different way of reporting status updates
  - New way to run meetings

* Why EOS?
- Designed for 10-250 person companies
- Need to be open-minded and growth-oriented
- More than 50k companies run on EOS
- EOS is simple and complete and helps companies grow and achieve goals / vision
- Everybody is rowing in the same direction
- Eliminate time-wasting actives due to miscommunication
- EOS saves time

* What's in It for Me?
- EOS helps you
  - Work more effectively
  - Less frustration
  - Clear understanding of connection between your efforts and success of the
    company

# 2, How Does EOS Work?

* 6 Key Components of a Company on EOS
- Every company is comprised of 6 key components
  - Vision
  - People
  - Data
  - Issues
  - Process
  - Traction

- EOS requires open and honest communication

* The Vision Component
- Goal is to have everyone move in the same direction so that energy is not
  wasted
  - Avoid running around in circles, make the same mistakes over and over
  - More gets done in less time
  - Work is more fun

* Tools for Vision Component
- Vision/Tracker Organizer (VTO) is the tool to keep everyone in the company
  aligned on the same short / long term goals
  - It requires to answer 8 questions
  - Make sure the company agrees and buys in to the answers of the 8 questions

* The People Component
- You need to put "the right people in the right seats"
  - Persons who consistently excels at a clearly defined, necessary job in the
    organization
- When the company has 100% of the Right people in the Right seats
  - Work is done more efficiently
  - There are fewer fires to put out
  - Mistakes are rare
  - Communication is better
  - The Company is truly a great place to work

* The Right People
- The "right people" are:
  - The ones who just fit
  - People you like to work with
  - People who want to accomplish something every day
  - They sacrifice so that the team
  - They act according to the company's core values

* The Right Seats
- The "right seats" are the roles needed to best contribute to the organization

* Tools for People Component
- Accountability Chart
- People Analyzer

- To answer the questions
  - Am I right person for this Company?
  - Do I fit?
  - Am I in the right seat?
  - Can I do my job really well?

* The Data Component

- Many companies fly blind all the time, like a plane with no gauges and no
  radar

* Tools for Data Component
- Scorecards
  - Handful of numbers (i.e., Measurables) that tell you how you are doing and
    where you are going
  - Each department and team should have a Scorecard
- Measurables
  - One person should be accountable for each of it
  - E.g.,
    - Customer sale meetings
    - Units produced
  - Each person should have a Measurable and a goal for that number
- Pros
  - You know how you are doing
  - Your manager knows what you have accomplished
  - The team can see if you are on track
  - You can find problems become a crisis

* The Issue Component
- Issues are things that can be improved, fixed, or changed to make your company
  better, e.g.,
  - Anything unresolved that needs to be discussed
  - Problems
  - Opportunities
  - New and better ways of doing things
- E.g.,
  - Sell more
  - Reduce rework
  - Fix inventory problems

- Seeing problems but not solving them leads to finger-pointing, dysfunction,
  frustration on both managers and employees

- Everybody role is to bring up issues as they see them and help solve them

* Tools for Issue Component
- Issues list
  - Allow everyone to openly and honestly list all unresolved issues
- Issues solving track
  - Disciplined way to help prioritize and solve issues

* The Process Component
- Create at a high level a consistent way of doing all the company's operations,
  e.g.,
  - All the projects are managed in the same way
  - How you make your product or service
  - How salespeople sell your product (from generating leads to closing the
    deals)
  - How to serve your customers so that they are always satisfied
  - How the Company bills and collects money

- Following the same process:
  - Saves an incredible amount of time
  - Eliminates frustration
  - Help to perform the job with fewer mistakes and delays

- Everyone follows the processes, creating consistency and efficiency

- Using processes the company can grow and scale in a manageable way

- Once all processes for repetitious tasks are uniform, energy can be put to
  creative tasks and solve the unexpected

* The Traction Component

- In a company with no traction
  - People in your company are not accountable
  - Things don't get done on time
  - Due dates are constantly missed
  - Your Company takes on too much

- The goal of the Traction component is to:
  - Get projects completed on time
  - People who commit to a deadline hit it
  - People put the extra effort needed and don't want to let one another down
  - People can't make excuses for being unproductive and so leave
  - Everyone focuses on only a few priorities for the next 90 days (which are
    tied to the company's overall goals)

* Tools for the Traction Component
- Rocks
  - Most important things that need to get done in the next 90 days
- Level 10 meetings
  - Have an agenda
  - Solve issues
  - Allow the team to stay connected

* Summary

| Component    | What's it all about                                     | Tool(s)                               | Your Role                                                   |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vision**   | Getting everyone aligned and focused on the same vision | V/TO                                  | Understand and buy into the answers to the 8 V/TO questions |
| **People**   | Getting the Right People in the Right Seats             | Accountability Chart, People Analyzer | Ask yourself if you are the Right Person in the Right Seat  |
| **Data**     | Measuring performance and having a pulse                | Scorecard, Measurables                | Establish and achieve your Measurables                      |
| **Issues**   | Identifying and solving issues                          | Issues List, Issues Solving Track     | Bring up and help solve issues                              |
| **Process**  | Documenting the way you do things and being consistent  | Documented Core Process               | Learn, follow, and help improve the Core Processes          |
| **Traction** | Accountability, discipline, and execution               | Meeting Pulse, Rocks                  | Complete your Rocks and engage in meetings                  |

# 3, Do You See What They Are Saying?

* The Vision
- Every Company should have a crystal clear vision, which is written down and
  shared with everyone
  - Only in this way every one rows in the same direction
  - Otherwise there is poor communication, confusion, wasted effort

- Sometimes
  - Leaders carry the vision in their heads
  - Leaders believe everyone agree on the same vision, and they really don't

* V/TO
- Vision/Traction Organizer
- The goal is have a shared map the for where the Company has been, is, and is
  going

| V/TO Question      | What it answers                                               |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Core Values        | Who you are                                                   |
| Core Focus         | What you are                                                  |
| 10-Year Target     | Where you are going                                           |
| Marketing Strategy | Which potential customers you are targeting with what message |
| 3-Year Picture     | What you will look like in 3 years                            |
| 1-Year Plan        | What your goals are for the next 12 months                    |
| Rocks              | What your priorities are for the next 90 days                 |
| Issues             | What will stop you or get you there faster                    |

* V/TO 1: What Are Your Core Values?
- Core values
  - Are a timeless set of guiding principles
  - Define the culture, who fits and who doesn't
  - Define what makes the Company different and unique

- In a Company, culture wins in the long term
- A Company needs to know who it is

- If people share the core values

* Core Values: Examples
- Be humble but confident
- Grow or die
- Help first
- Do what you say
  - E.g., if you commit to a date, you hit it
  - You fully deliver and finish what you start: no half-assed things
  - If you can't do it, don't commit
- Do the right thing

* V/TO 2: What Is Your Core Focus?
- Core Focus is about:
  - Keeping your company focused on the areas where your business excels
  - Not getting distracted by the "shiny stuff"

- You can't be everything to everyone

- You need to have a Niche: something you do better than anyone else

* V/TO 3: What Is Your 10-Year Target?
- 10-Year Target
  - Is your long-term, larger-than-life goal
  - Unites everyone around one common objective

* V/TO 4: What Is Your Marketing Strategy?
- Clarify
  - Who your ideal customer is
  - The most appealing message to them

- Then laser-focus on it and do now waste money and effort
  - Trying to be everting to everyone
  - With inconsistent marketing message
  - Taking trips to prospective customers who are not a good fit for the company
    or are not going to say yes
  - Committing to projects in which they don't have expertise

* Marketing Strategy
- It has 4 key elements
  - Target market
    - Characteristics of your best customers
      - E.g., who they are, where they are, how they think
    - Focus on customers who is best to do business with
    - E.g.,
      - $5-50M in revenue
      - 20-250 employees
      - Southeastern US
      - Growth-oriented
      - Seeking a partner, not a commodity provider
  - 3 Uniques
    - Things that make you better than the competition and why your customers
      should buy from you
    - Consistent messaging market for sales and marketing efforts
    - E.g.,
      - 99% on-time delivery
      - Local technicians
      - 24hr response time
  - Proven process
    - A visual of what you do to deliver value to your customers
    - What your customers will experience working with you
  - Guarantee
    - It says you stand behind your product or service
    - Put your clients at ease
    - E.g.,
      - "We will hit our ship date, or will pay for shipping"
      - "If we don't add value, you don't pay"
      - "We will show up on time, or the first hour is free"

* V/TO 5: What Is Your 3-Year Picture?
- Paint a picture of what your company will look like in 3 years
  - Revenue
  - Profit
  - A Measurable
  - 5-10 bullets to paint a vivid image of what your Company will look like,
    feel like, be like in 3 years

- E.g.,
  - Revenue: $15m
  - Profit: $2m
  - Measurable: 300 customers
  - What does it look like?
    - 80 employees
    - 3 new product lines
    - 100% Right people in the Right seats
    - Strong culture
    - Largest customer accounts for <10% of revenue
    - All Core processes documented and followed by all

- It needs to be written down and shared with everyone

* V/TO 6: What Is Your 1-Year Plan?
- Revenue target
- Profit target
- Measurable
- Top 3-7 goals for the year
  - Fewer is better
  - With too many things to do, people lose focus and accomplish very few of
    them

- This will put you on a path to reach the 3-year picture and the 10-year target

* V/TO 7: What Are Your Rocks?
- Rocks are the 3-7 most important objectives the company must get done in the
  next 90 days (i.e., one quarter) to achieve the 1-year plan

- The 90 days is to keep people focused and energetic on the priorities
  - There are always unplanned emergencies, day-to-day distractions
  - Then plans start to unravel
  - You need to break the overall goals into smaller and manageable chunks

- After 90 days, you start losing focus and new set of Rocks will refocus the
  Company

* V/TO 8: What Are Your Company's Issues?
- To achieve the vision you need to admit all of your company's obstacles,
  everything that can prevent the company from reaching your vision

- Every company has issues
  - No one admits them
  - Everyone might talk about it but does nothing

# 4, Who's Doing What?

* Without Roles and Responsibilities
- In an organization where roles and responsibilities are unclear
  - People trip over each other
  - Everyone is working hard but running in circles
  - Accomplish very little
  - Finger-pointing

- The right structure will:
  - Clarify roles and responsibilities
  - Create clear reporting lines
  - Facilitate efficient decision making
  - Help people get things done
  - Enable communication
  - Identity all the available seats in the organization

* Accountability
- All-for-one-and-for-all approach works only for small (< 10 people) startups
  - People wear many hats to keep the company survive

- As a Company mature, the all-for-one approach becomes a roadblock to growth
  - People trip over each other
  - Everyone is still trying to do everything
  - Company stalls
  - Pressure and stress increase
  - The blame game starts

- No one knows who is responsible for what
- No one is held accountable for anything
- There are too many cooks in the kitchen

- You miss a customer deadline
  - Everyone points fingers

- You lose a major customer
  - 4 different people have 4 different explanations of what happened

- When everyone is accountable, no one is

* The Accountability chart
- It is an organizational chart that
  - Shows structure
  - Clarifies the roles and responsibilities (the "seat")
    - Five bullet points explaining
  - No confusing reporting lines from an employee to multiple bosses
  - Establishes clear ownership and accountability for everything

- There is no "right" accountability chart because every company is different
  - The accountability chart is specific of your company based on size, growth
    goals, culture, product, or service

# 5, What Is Most Important Right Now?

* Rocks: Intuition
- You have a small pile of rocks, some pebbles, and some sand
- Your objective is to fit them all in a glass jar
- If you put the sand first and then the pebbles, the rocks don't fit
- If you put the rocks first and then the pebbles and the sand (which filters
  through the bigger things), it fits

* Rocks
- The rocks represent the most important things you must get done
- The pebbles are your day-to-day responsibilities
- The sand represents all the interruptions during the day

- If you don't prioritize your Rocks (the most important things first), you will
  not get them done

* The 90-Day World
- Human mind tends to lose focus

- Every 90 days
  - you get together with your team
  - review the Rocks that were accomplished
  - Set the Rocks for the next 90 days

- Everything else (things you don't have capacity, things that are not as
  important) is pushed to the next period, minimizing wasted effort,
  talking too much

- The problem:
  - Work on a project for a few weeks
  - The manager says that it wasn't a priority any more
  - Assign a new project

* Your Rocks
- Each individual should have 1-3 Rocks every 90 days
- Rocks should be aligned with your company or your team's Rocks
  - E.g.,
    - If your team Rock is "Purchase 4 new trucks"
    - Your Rock could be "Find 10 potential trucks to buy that match the needed
      specification"
- Rocks should be tied to your day-to-day responsibilities
  - E.g., for a salesperson the Rock is "schedule 20 sales appointments"

* SMART
- Each Rock needs to be SMART
  - Specific
    - Is it clear enough?
  - Measurable
    - Can you measure (with a number) the completion of your Rock?
  - Attainable
    - Can you achieve it?
  - Realistic
    - Can you complete the Rock in 90 days?
  - Timely
    - Is this the right time?

* SMART vs non-SMART Rocks
- Bad: Safety inspection
- Good: Pass 100% of safety inspections

- Bad: Online class
- Good: Successfully complete an online training class on website marketing

* Reviewing Rocks Weekly
- You need to check on the team during weekly meetings
- Every team needs to report if the team is "on track" or "off track"
  - This creates a new language to hold people accountable
  - People should not take it personally

- Completing 80% of the Rocks every 90 days is a great job

# 6, Why Do We Have to Have Meetings?

* Meetings
- Meetings are not a waste of time
  - It's what you do in meetings that's a waste of time

- Meeting should start and end on time
  - Provide input on which issues to tackle
  - Participate in the decisions and the solutions

- Meetings are necessary

* How to End Procrastination
- Human beings by nature procrastinate
  - Wait until the last minute to get things done

- To solve procrastination you need to give a "pulse" to the organization

* Weekly Meeting Pulse
- EOS recommends a weekly meeting pulse
  - Since there is a spike of activity more often, there is no time to
    procrastinate

- The goal is
  - Minimize number and length of meetings
  - Maximize meeting productivity

- The "right meeting pulse" is to accomplish 4 things
  - Make sure everything is on track
  - Keep the team connected
  - Hold each other accountable
  - Solve issues

- The meeting
  - Is always on the same day and same time
  - Starts and ends on time
  - Is 90 mins long

* Level 10 Meetings

* Roles in L10 Meetings
- Meeting facilitator:
  - Runs the meeting
  - Move the team through the agenda
  - Keep everything on track
- Scribe:
  - Bring the list of Rocks, Scorecard, Agenda
  - Update the documents
- There should be two different persons
  - It's difficult to play both roles

* L10 Agenda
- Wins / good news (5 mins)
- Scorecard (5 mins)
- Rocks review (5 mins)
- Customer / employee headlines (5 mins)
- Todo list (5 mins)
  - ...
- Identity-discuss-solve (60 mins)
  - ...
- Conclusion (5 min)

* Wins
- Each person shares one piece of good news from the past week
  - Help humanize the process
  - Allow team members to get to know each other
  - Start the meeting off with a positive vibe

* Scorecard
- Make sure your Measurables (KPIs, metrics) are on track
  - You should just review, no questions, no commentary, no excuses
  - You either hit the goal for the week or you didn't
- There is no discussion
  - Any comment is dropped won to the Issues List
  - Simply say "Drop it down"
  - You want to avoid a painful reporting session of why and how the numbers are
    bad

* Rock Review
- Make sure that each person's Rocks are on track
  - The facilitator reads the list of Rocks one at a time
  - The Rock owner states if it's on track or not
    - No questions, no commentary, now excuses
    - Any question needs to get dropped down to the Issues List

- It takes discipline

* Customer / Employee Headlines
- Everyone can bring up good or bad news about customers, employees, or both
  - 1-2 sentence headline
  - If it takes more, drop it down

* TODOs
- These are the commitments made by people in the last L10 meeting
  - If something was off-track last week, it was decided to be fixed
  - Was it done?

- TODOs are 7 day action items
- The goal is to complete 90% of them each week

* IDS
- This is were you solve all your key issues
- You are going to have 60 mins to solve the issues

- There are 3 types of issues
  - Decisions
    - E.g., "customer XYZ is not happy with the level of customer support"
  - Information to share
    - E.g., "we are having our 2 largest customers into the office this week"
  - Information is needed
    - E.g., "Roger needs to know when the next software release will be"

- The goal is to
  - solve the most important issues well (so they don't come back!)
  - not to solve all the issues on your list

- If you run out of time, the other issues can stay on the list until next week

* IDS
- IDS is the issues solving tracking tool
  - Identify
  - Discuss
  - Solve

- Meetings are a waste of time if teams spend all their time discussing an issue
  - They just talk and talk and feel productive
  - You need to clearly identify the issue

* Who, Who, 1-Sentence
- To clearly identify the issue
  - Who: is raising the issue
  - Who: is accountable for solving the issue
  - 1-sentence: state the issue in one sentence by stating the problem directly
    to her (short, sweet, no candy-coating)

- Bob tells Sue "I'm not getting reports from you on time"
  - Then there is a productive conversation about the problem and how to solve it

* Tangent Alert
- People start getting off the subject and going down rabbit holes
  - Simple say "tangent alert!"

* Conclusion
- With 5 mins left in the meeting, even if you are in the middle of an issue,
  you need to do 3 things to end the meeting on time

- Recap the todo list
- Decide if there are important messages to communicate inside / outside the
  company based on the decisions you have made
- Rate the meting

* Rate the Meeting
- Did it start and end on time?
- Did you follow the L10 agenda?
- Is everyone on the same page?
- Did 90% or more of the todos get done?
- Did you solve the most important issues?

- The minimum average rating should be 8
  - If somebody ranks the meeting below an 8 you should ask "what would have made
    the meeting a 10 for you?"
  - Collect answers on how to improve

# 7, What's My Number?

* Without a Scorecard
- Companies work hard but don't know how they are doing
- People
  - Are frustrated
  - Are unmotivated
  - Perform poorly
- The result is:
  - Wasted time
  - Poor resource allocation
  - Weak overall performance
  - Terrible results

* The Scorecard
- The Scorecard:
  - Is a handful of numbers that how the company or your department are doing
  - Tells what is working and what is not
  - Solves issues before they become major disasters
  - Eliminate assumptions and egos
  - Creates transparency, accountability

* Effect of the Scorecard
- Everybody knows exactly where they are
- Numbers are reviewed and tracked weekly
- Everybody is constantly striving to hit their target
- If people go off track, within one week the problem is discussed as IDS and
  fixed
- Everybody is accountable

- "What gets measure gets done"
- "If you shoot for the moon, you get to where nobody else can reach"

* A Typical Scorecard
- The Company has a scorecard with 5-15 numbers
- Each department has a scorecard with 3-5 numbers

- All scorecards for Company and Departments are reviewed weekly in the L10
  meeting

- Usually you want to produce current activity that produces future results,
  e.g.,
  - Utilization
  - Number of sales meetings
  - Average time to resolve complaints
  - Number of bugs fixed

- Each activity should be tied to future results, such as revenues, sales, and
  profits
  - E.g., achieve $10M in revenue
  - 5 new clients per week, which is $200,000 in sales per week
  - You need to schedule 20 sales meetings per week

* Scorecards: Columns
- Each scorecard should have 4 key columns
  - Who: person accountable for the number
    - The person who can most influence the number
    - Not the one who reports (e.g., a manager)
  - Measurables: 3-5 numbers
  - Goal: the number that must be hit every week
  - Date: week we are reporting for

* Everyone's Number
- Everyone should have a number
- A number
  - Cuts through the subjective communication
  - Creates accountability
    - Expectations are clear
  - Creates commitment
  - Creates competition
  - Creates the right kind of pressure and peer-pressure
  - Produce results
  - Solves problem faster
    - If an activity is off track, the problem can be recognized and solved
- Right people in the right seats appreciate numbers
  - "Wrong people in the wrong seats resist Measurables"

# 8, How Am I Doing?

* Right People in the Right Seats
- Great organizations are built by having 100% "the Right people in the Right
  seats"

- Right people are the ones who fit in the Company's culture and share the Core
  Values
  - People should have job where their innate talents and skills are put to the
    best use

- Each seat has 5 roles and responsibilities, e.g.,
  - Solve customer issues
  - Document customer interactions
  - Escalate when appropriate
  - ...

* People Analyzer: Step 1
- For each person you need to rate how they do with respect to Company's Core
  Values
- Match the Core Values
  - `+`: most of the time
  - `+/-`: sometimes
  - `-`: rarely

- Then you need to set "the bar", i.e., the minimum acceptable score to accept
  - Recommended 3 `+` and 2 `+/-`

* People Analyzer: Step 2
- You can use 3 criteria called "GWC" to understand if each person is in the
  Right Seat

- Do they *get* the job?
  - Do they understand all the ins and outs of the position?
  - Do they understand how their job relates to the other jobs?
  - Are they "wired" for that job?
  - E.g., a flight attendants who is rude, a salesperson who don't understand the
    product after months of training

- Do they *want* the job?
  - Do they get up every morning wanting to do the job?
  - Do they show passion for it?

- Do they have the *capacity* to do the job?
  - Do they have the ability and the time to do the job?
  - E.g., a pest-control field rep who is scared of bugs

- For these questions there are only yes or no, no maybes
  - The bar is 3 yeses

* People Analyzer: Step 3
- Apply the People Analyzer to yourself and your seat
  - You need to be completely honest and open

- Sometimes the problem is just being in the wrong seat
  - People are often unhappy when in the wrong seat

* The Quarterly Conversation
- Employees often report that they would love more feedback and opportunities to
  discuss how they are doing
- Every manager should have a quarterly conversation with their direct reports

- You should compare notes
- You can use the People Analyzer
- It should not be a blame session
- Everybody has things that are not working and can be improved

- Informal 1-hour one-on-one meeting to
  - Talk about what's working and what's not
  - Clarify expectations
  - Communicate well
  - Make course corrections

* Questions About What Works
- What am I most proud of accomplishing in the last quarter?
- What changes did I make to accomplish my job?
- What did I do well to hit my Rocks and Measurables?
- Am I working on projects and in a role that matters?
- Do I demonstrate the Company's Core Values?

* Questions About What Doesn'T Work
- What makes my job more difficult?
- What processes / procedures are broken?
- What could I have done differently?
- How could my manager help me being more successful?
- What's not working?

- A good or bad Annual Performance Review should never be a surprise

# 9, What Do I Do Next?

* Implementing EOS
- Implementing EOS in your Company is a lifelong effort

- We have a clear vision who is shared by everyone
- Our core values are clear
- We use our core values to hire, review, reward, and fire
- Our Core Focus is clear
- Our 10-year target is clear and shared
- Our target market is clear and all marketing and sales efforts
