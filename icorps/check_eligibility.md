## Step 1
Read the markdown file and compute the answers to the following questions

- What is the applying school?
  - E.g., John Hopkins, UMD, ...

- Is HCT and or NSF Lineage?
  - A team is HCT (Hub-connected team) also known as "Hub recommended team" based
    on the answer to the question: **Which program best describes your team?**
  - A team is NSF Lineage if it has received previous NSF research awards

- Is the innovation deep-tech?

- Did the team do regional?

- How many interviews in the I-Corps regional?

- Are the Entrepreneurial Lead (EL), co-EL, Technical Lead (TL), co-TL, Principal
  Investigator (PI) of the same schools
  - Check if they have emails from the right school
  - Print a summary of the roles, their job (e.g., student, professor) and the
    email

- Are EL(s) STEM students, with a PhD or MS?

- Are EL(s) and TL associated to the laboratory of the PI?

- Is the PI on record?
  - A PI is on record if it's not also a technical lead

- Was the IP disclosed to TTO?

- Is Industry Mentor a good fit?
  - An industry mentor is a good fit, if it's not affiliated directly to the school
    and it has expertise on business (e.g., MBA)

- Short description of the project
  - Print 20 words

- Sector:
  - E.g., AI

## Step 2
Write a file eligibility.txt with the answers to this questions without using
markdown tags and using spaces to align the questions and the answers

Do not put references to the document, just the answers
